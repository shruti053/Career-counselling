# Decision-Making & Perspective Questionnaire (10th Standard)

**Instructions:** Read each situation and choose the option that feels most natural to you. All choices are reasonable — there are no right or wrong answers. Pick what you would genuinely do in that moment.

---

### Q1. Your friend asks for help with homework the night before it's due. You have your own work to finish.

- **A)** Share your completed work so they can use it as a reference
- **B)** Spend an hour explaining the difficult parts and let them finish the rest
- **C)** Tell them you're busy tonight but offer to help them the next day after school
- **D)** Suggest they check the textbook examples since the homework is based on those

---

### Q2. During a class debate, a student makes a point that is factually incorrect, but the teacher doesn't notice.

- **A)** Raise your hand and politely correct the information
- **B)** Note it down and speak to the teacher privately after class

- **D)** Ask a follow-up question that gently leads the class to notice the error

## How to Interpret Responses

Each option reflects a **natural tendency**, not a measure of intelligence. Here's what patterns suggest:

### Tendency Toward Direct Action (Often A-type choices)
You prefer to handle situations yourself, take initiative, and act rather than wait. You value efficiency and getting things done. You may sometimes act before considering all angles.

### Tendency Toward Thoughtful Approach (Often B-type choices)
You like to process information, consider options, and act with intention. You value understanding before acting. You may sometimes overthink or delay decisions.

### Tendency Toward Collaborative/Delegative (Often C or D-type choices)
You believe in involving others, respecting boundaries, and finding middle ground. You value harmony and shared responsibility. You may sometimes avoid taking a firm stance.

### Key Observation Areas:

| Situation Type | What It Reveals |
|---|---|
| Q1, Q9, Q13, Q16, Q19 | **Time & Priority Management** — How you balance competing demands |
| Q2, Q6, Q12, Q15, Q17, Q20 | **Social Judgment** — How you navigate interpersonal gray areas |
| Q3, Q5, Q11, Q14, Q18 | **Responsibility & Leadership** — How you handle obligations and authority |
| Q4, Q7, Q8, Q10 | **Self-Awareness & Empathy** — How you relate to yourself and others |

### Important Note:
> This is not a test with scores. Two people can choose differently in the same situation and both responses can be perfectly reasonable. The value lies in **discussing** the choices — asking "why did you pick that?" reveals far more than the answer itself.

---

## Suggested Discussion Prompts

After the questionnaire, ask:

1. *"Which question was hardest for you to answer and why?"*
2. *"Did any question make you think about a situation differently?"*
3. *"If you could change one of your answers after thinking more, which one and what would you change it to?"*
4. *"Is there a situation from your own life that was similar to any of these questions?"*

---

*The purpose of this questionnaire is to start a conversation, not to reach a conclusion. Every answer tells a story about how a person sees the world — and that story is worth listening to.*
