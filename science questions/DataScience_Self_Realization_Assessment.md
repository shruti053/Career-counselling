# Data Science Self-Realization Assessment
## For Students of Classes 10–12 Considering Data Science

**Purpose:** This assessment helps you discover how you naturally notice patterns, question information, handle uncertainty, and make sense of evidence. It is designed to create self-awareness about whether data science thinking aligns with your natural style. There are no "right" or "wrong" answers in the traditional sense — every choice reveals something meaningful about how your mind works.

**Instructions:** Read each question carefully. Choose the option that feels most natural to you. Take your time. Reflect on why you chose what you did.

**Total Questions: 40**

## SECTION A — Mathematical Thinking & Pattern Discovery (Questions 1-6)

**Question 1**  
**Category:** Mathematical Thinking & Pattern Discovery  

A small shop records the number of visitors each month for six months: 120, 135, 150, 165, 180, 195. Purchases per visitor remain roughly the same. What pattern seems most worth investigating?

A. The steady increase in visitors suggests something is attracting more people over time.  
B. The numbers are increasing, so sales must be increasing at the same rate.  
C. The pattern is random and not worth further attention.  
D. The shop should stop tracking visitors because the numbers keep changing.  

**Correct Answer:** A  

**Why This Question Matters:** Reveals whether you look beyond the surface increase to consider what might be driving the trend.  

**Insight Being Measured:** Pattern Recognition and Trend Investigation

## How to Use This Assessment

After completing all 40 questions:

1. Review your chosen options across sections.
2. Notice patterns in your choices (e.g., do you naturally look for patterns, question conclusions, seek missing context, or feel comfortable with probabilistic thinking?).
3. Reflect on the "Insight Being Measured" for questions where your choice surprised you.
4. Consider: Does the way you observe, question, and interpret information match the thinking style often found in data science?

This assessment is meant to spark self-reflection. Discuss your responses with a teacher, parent, or counselor for deeper insights.

**Remember:** Data Science is not just about numbers or coding. It is about noticing patterns, questioning assumptions, making sense of uncertainty, and using evidence to understand the world more clearly.
